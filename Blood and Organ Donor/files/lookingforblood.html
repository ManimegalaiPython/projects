Why Your Blood Group Is Important

Did You Know We Have Two Main Blood Group Systems Which Help Decide Who Gets Your Blood? The ABO System And The Rh(D) Type.

The Combination Of These Two Systems Give Us The Eight Commonly-Known Blood Types: A+, A-, B+, B-, O+, O-, AB+, AB-.

Knowing The Right Blood Type To Give A Patient Can Prevent Complications Following A Blood Transfusion For Doctors And Surgeons.

Do You Know What Blood Type You Are? Check Out The Givers/Receivers Chart To See Who You're Compatible With.

What's my type?

Your blood type is inherited from your biological parents, similar to how your hair and eye colour are determined. It all comes down to genetics!

ABO Blood Group System

A and B genes, within the ABO system, are co-dominant, meaning that they will be expressed wherever the gene is present. However, the O gene will only be expressed in the absence of an A or B gene.

Rh(D) Blood Group System

The Rh(D) gene is also dominant and will be expressed if inherited from either parent. If you do not carry the Rh(D) gene you will be Rh(D) negative.
